India's higher education system serves over 40 million students, yet a critical disconnect persists between the National Education Policy 2020's mandate for technology-integrated instruction and the ground-level reality where educators lack the technical proficiency to create multimedia learning resources—while video-based learning has emerged as one of the most effective modalities for knowledge transfer, the technical barriers of video editing, audio production, and content optimization remain prohibitively high, creating an inequitable landscape where only technically adept instructors can leverage this powerful pedagogical tool. This paper introduces a YouTube AI Agent framework that democratizes educational video content creation through a multi-agent architecture comprising four specialized components: a Research Agent that analyzes successful educational videos to extract proven pedagogical patterns; a Script Agent that transforms these insights into structured, evidence-based scripts aligned with Mayer's multimedia learning principles; a Text-to-Speech Engine supporting multiple Indian languages including Hindi; and a Video Creator that automates production workflows. Grounded in constructivist philosophy and Freire's liberatory education principles, the framework positions AI as an equalizer that empowers educators to engage dialogically with the broader educational community while maintaining their unique contextual understanding of student needs. We demonstrate the framework's efficacy through an illustrative application in Python programming pedagogy, showing how educators can leverage successful content patterns to create professional-quality videos within hours rather than weeks, while preserving pedagogical agency through three flexible creation pathways accommodating varying levels of technical comfort and content readiness. By addressing the technical barriers that prevent widespread adoption of video-based teaching methods, this framework contributes to realizing NEP 2020's vision of quality education for all, particularly in resource-constrained environments where graceful degradation ensures functionality despite limited connectivity or computational resources.

Keywords: AI in education, YouTube pedagogy, constructivism, educational technology, Indian higher education, multi-agent systems, content democratization, computational thinking, teacher empowerment
